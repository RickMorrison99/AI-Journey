# Strategy & Adoption Frameworks

Strategic AI adoption requires mapping the landscape before choosing tools. It is easy to adopt AI capabilities reactively — chasing the latest model release or copying what a competitor announced. It is harder, and far more valuable, to understand *where* a given tool sits in its maturity lifecycle, *what* organisational capability is needed to use it well, and *how* to sequence investment so that early experiments build toward durable capability rather than one-off demonstrations.

This section provides two complementary frameworks for O&A's AI adoption strategy:

- **Wardley Mapping** answers the question of *what to build, buy, or consume* — and crucially, *when*. It provides a visual method for positioning AI tools on an evolution axis and deriving strategic actions from their position.
- **Ng's AI Transformation Playbook** answers the question of *how to build the organisational capability* to execute on those strategic choices — from running the right pilots to training the whole team, not just the enthusiasts.

Together, they provide O&A with both a landscape view and an execution model.

## How They Fit Together

Wardley and Ng are solving different problems, and both are necessary.

**Wardley tells you *what* to do.** It gives you situational awareness: which AI components are mature enough to buy, which are ripe for custom investment, and which are still too uncertain for anything other than time-boxed experiments. Without this awareness, organisations invest in the wrong things at the wrong time — building what they should buy, or buying what they should be pioneering.

**Ng tells you *how* to build the capability to do it.** Once the map tells you that custom YANG pipeline development is a strategic investment and GitHub Copilot is a product to subscribe to, Ng's playbook tells you how to sequence pilots, grow in-house expertise, train the whole team (not just the builders), and ensure the data foundations are in place before deploying AI at scale.

Use Wardley to decide *which* AI components O&A should invest in and at what level. Use Ng's playbook to build the team capability, organisational habit, and data infrastructure to execute on those decisions effectively. Neither framework alone is sufficient. Both together provide a strategy that is grounded in landscape reality and executable through deliberate organisational development.
